The Freelegal leaderboard provides public recognition for law firms that contribute pro bono services, creating a competitive environment that encourages participation and celebrates community impact.

## Overview

The leaderboard ranks law firms based on a point system that rewards various forms of engagement with the pro bono network. It appears in the account manager dashboard, providing visibility into firm participation.

<Note>
  The leaderboard is visible to account managers to help them identify active, engaged firms when assigning cases.
</Note>

## Point system

Firms earn points through multiple activities, rewarding both commitment and completed work:

## How to earn points

### Completing cases (+100 points)

This is the primary way to earn substantial points:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Accept case assignment">
    Express interest in a case and wait for account manager assignment.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Provide representation">
    Successfully represent the client and resolve their legal matter.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Close the case">
    Mark the case as completed in the system.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Earn recognition">
    Receive 100 points and move up the leaderboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Expressing interest (+25 points)

Earn points simply by indicating your firm's willingness to help:

1. Browse available cases in your firm dashboard
2. Review case details to ensure it matches your expertise
3. Click "Express Interest" on suitable cases
4. Receive 25 points immediately

<Note>
  You earn these points even if you're not ultimately assigned the case. This rewards firms for being engaged and responsive.
</Note>

### Ongoing representation (+50 points/month)

For longer-term cases:

- Points are automatically awarded at the end of each month of active representation
- Encourages firms to commit to complex, long-running cases
- Recognizes the sustained effort required for certain legal matters

### Referrals (+10 points)

When you can't take a case:

1. Identify another lawyer or firm who might be a better fit
2. Make the referral through the platform
3. Earn 10 points for supporting the network

<Warning>
  Only express interest in cases you can realistically take. Frequently withdrawing interest may impact your firm's reputation despite the points earned.
</Warning>

## Leaderboard structure

The leaderboard displays firm rankings in a table format with four columns:

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Rank** | Numerical ranking (#1, #2, #3, etc.) based on total points |
| **Firm** | Full firm name as registered in the system |
| **Points** | Total accumulated points from all activities |
| **Cases** | Number of completed cases |

## Leaderboard features

### Automatic sorting

The leaderboard automatically sorts firms by total points in descending order. If two firms have the same points, they're ranked by the number of completed cases.

### Real-time updates

As firms earn points through their activities, the leaderboard updates to reflect new rankings.

### Visual hierarchy

The leaderboard uses visual design to emphasize rankings:
- Rank numbers are displayed in bold
- Top firms appear at the top of the table
- Clear column headers for easy scanning

## Maximizing your firm's leaderboard position

### Best practices

1. **Express interest frequently** - Earn 25 points per case even if not assigned
2. **Complete cases thoroughly** - The 100-point reward is substantial
3. **Commit to ongoing cases** - Monthly points add up over time
4. **Make referrals** - Help the network and earn points when you can't help directly
5. **Be consistent** - Regular participation maintains high rankings

### Strategic approach

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start with quick wins">
    Express interest in multiple cases to build initial points quickly.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Take on manageable cases">
    Accept and complete shorter cases for the 100-point completion bonus.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Build ongoing commitments">
    Take on some longer-term cases for recurring monthly points.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Support the network">
    Make referrals when cases don't fit your expertise.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The leaderboard rewards both quantity and quality. Firms that complete cases successfully earn more recognition than those who only express interest.
</Note>

## Viewing the leaderboard

The leaderboard is accessible through the account manager dashboard:

1. Log in as an account manager
2. Navigate to the manager dashboard
3. Scroll down below the case tables
4. View the "Leaderboard" section with current rankings

<Warning>
  The leaderboard is currently only visible to account managers. Legal representatives can track their own points but cannot see comparative rankings of other firms.
</Warning>
