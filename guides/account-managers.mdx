This guide is for law students and volunteers who serve as account managers on the Freelegal platform. As an account manager, you play a crucial role in connecting clients with appropriate legal representation.

## What is an account manager?

Account managers are typically law students or legal volunteers who:

- Review submitted cases to understand legal issues and client needs
- Evaluate which legal representatives are best suited for each case
- Assign cases to lawyers and law firms who have expressed interest
- Monitor case progress and ensure clients receive appropriate help
- Manage the case pipeline to ensure timely responses

<Note>
  This is a volunteer position that provides valuable experience in case management, legal assessment, and connecting clients with appropriate representation.
</Note>

## Registering as an account manager

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the registration page">
    Visit the Freelegal website and click "Register" in the navigation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select your user type">
    Choose "Law student / Account manager" as your registration type. This option is selected by default on the registration page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Fill in your information">
    Provide:
    - Full name
    - Email address
    - Password (and confirm it)
    - School Name (optional but recommended)
    
    All required fields are marked with a red asterisk (*).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete registration">
    Click "Register" to create your account. Once approved, you'll gain access to the Manager Dashboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Include your law school name in your profile. This helps the Freelegal team verify your volunteer credentials and may provide networking opportunities with other student volunteers.
</Tip>

## Accessing the manager dashboard

After logging in, you'll be directed to the Manager Dashboard (also called the Case Portal). This is your central hub for managing pro bono cases.

The dashboard displays:

- **Available cases**: New submissions that need review and assignment
- **Assigned cases**: Cases you've already matched with legal representatives
- **Case details**: Title, category, submission date, and current status
- **Action buttons**: Options to view case details and assign to representatives
- **Leaderboard**: Track which firms and representatives are most active

## Understanding the dashboard interface

The Case Portal has two main tabs:

### Available cases tab

Shows all new case submissions that haven't been assigned yet. Each case displays:

- **Case ID**: Unique identifier for the submission
- **Title**: Brief description of the legal issue
- **Category**: Type of legal matter (Housing/Tenancy, Family Law, Employment, etc.)
- **Date**: When the case was submitted
- **Status**: Either "New" (just submitted) or "In Progress" (being reviewed)

### Assigned cases tab

Shows cases that you've matched with legal representatives. These are cases where a lawyer or firm has expressed interest and you've confirmed the assignment.

<Note>
  Cases move from "Available" to "Assigned" after you assign them to a legal representative who has expressed interest.
</Note>

## Reviewing submitted cases

When reviewing cases, your goal is to understand the legal issue and identify appropriate representation.

<Steps>
  <Step title="View case details">
    Click the "View" button next to any case to see the full submission, including:
    - Complete case description
    - Client contact information (if provided)
    - Legal category
    - Any additional context the client provided
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Assess the legal issue">
    Evaluate:
    - Type of legal matter and urgency
    - Complexity of the case
    - What kind of legal expertise is needed
    - Whether it falls within pro bono service areas
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check for expressed interest">
    Review which legal representatives have expressed interest in the case. The system shows firms and lawyers who have indicated they can help.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Identify the best match">
    Consider:
    - The representative's area of expertise
    - Their history of completed cases
    - Current caseload and availability
    - Geographic location if relevant
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Red flags to watch for:**
  - Cases outside typical pro bono scope
  - Insufficient information to assess the legal issue
  - Potential conflicts of interest
  - Issues that may require emergency intervention
  
  If you encounter these, consult with senior account managers or Freelegal administrators.
</Tip>

## Assigning cases to legal representatives

Once you've identified the right match, you can assign the case.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review expressed interest">
    Check which legal representatives have clicked "Express Interest" on the case. Only representatives who have expressed interest should receive assignments.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click the Assign button">
    In the "Available Cases" tab, click the yellow "Assign" button next to the case you want to assign.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm the assignment">
    The system will prompt you to confirm the assignment. Make sure you're assigning to the right representative.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Monitor the handoff">
    The case will move to your "Assigned Cases" tab. The legal representative will be notified and can begin working with the client.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Once assigned, the legal representative receives the client's contact information and takes responsibility for the case. You can still monitor progress but the representative handles direct client communication.
</Note>

## Best practices for case review

### Thorough assessment

- Read the entire case description carefully
- Look for urgency indicators (court dates, deadlines, immediate danger)
- Identify any special requirements (language needs, accessibility considerations)

### Timely response

- Review new submissions within 24-48 hours
- Prioritize urgent cases (evictions, restraining orders, pending court dates)
- Follow up with legal representatives after assignment

### Quality matching

- Match cases with representatives who have relevant expertise
- Consider workload - don't overwhelm active volunteers
- Look at the leaderboard to identify engaged representatives

### Communication

- If a case needs clarification, reach out to the client using provided contact info
- Communicate with legal representatives about case details
- Keep notes on case assignments for future reference

### Confidentiality

- Maintain strict confidentiality of all case information
- Only share client details with assigned legal representatives
- Follow all privacy protocols and data security practices

<Tip>
  **Building expertise:**
  - Start with straightforward cases in common categories (housing, employment)
  - Consult with experienced account managers on complex cases
  - Learn from case outcomes to improve your assessment skills
  - Track which assignments work well to inform future matches
</Tip>

## Managing case assignments

After assigning a case:

### Withdrawing assignments

If you need to withdraw an assignment (representative became unavailable, case reassessment needed):

1. Go to the "Assigned Cases" tab
2. Click "Withdraw assignment" next to the case
3. The case returns to "Available" and can be reassigned

### Monitoring progress

Keep track of assigned cases:

- Check that legal representatives have contacted clients
- Follow up if assignments haven't been acknowledged
- Be available to answer questions from representatives

## Understanding the leaderboard

The dashboard includes a leaderboard showing the most active legal representatives and firms. This gamification helps encourage participation.

**How points are earned:**

- **+25 points**: Expressing interest in a case
- **+50 points**: Each month of ongoing representation
- **+100 points**: Successfully completing a pro bono case
- **+10 points**: Referring a case to another lawyer when unable to take it

Use the leaderboard to:

- Identify the most engaged representatives
- Recognize top performers
- Find reliable matches for challenging cases
- Encourage friendly competition among volunteers

## Tips for effective case management

### Develop expertise in specific areas

As you review more cases, you'll develop expertise in certain legal categories. This helps you:

- Spot issues more quickly
- Make better matching decisions
- Provide helpful context to legal representatives

### Build relationships with representatives

Get to know the lawyers and firms in the system:

- Learn their areas of expertise
- Understand their preferences and availability
- Develop trust through consistent quality matches

### Stay organized

- Check the dashboard regularly
- Prioritize urgent cases
- Keep mental notes on cases you've assigned
- Follow up on assignments that seem stalled

### Continuous learning

Use this volunteer opportunity to:

- Learn about different areas of law
- Understand practical legal practice
- Develop case assessment skills
- Build your professional network

<Tip>
  Many successful account managers dedicate 5-10 hours per week to case review and management. Find a schedule that works for you and maintain consistency.
</Tip>

## Troubleshooting common scenarios

### No expressed interest

If a case has no expressed interest from legal representatives:

- Wait 48-72 hours for representatives to review
- Consider if the case description needs clarification
- Reach out to representatives who handle that type of case
- Consult with Freelegal administrators if it's an urgent matter

### Multiple interested representatives

When several representatives express interest:

- Review their profiles and expertise
- Check their current caseload on the leaderboard
- Consider geographic proximity if relevant
- Choose based on best fit for the client's needs

### Case requires immediate attention

For urgent cases:

- Mark the case for priority review
- Reach out directly to representatives who can respond quickly
- Contact Freelegal administrators if it's an emergency
- Follow up to ensure the client receives timely help
