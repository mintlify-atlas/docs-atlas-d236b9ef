This guide will walk you through the process of requesting legal assistance through the Freelegal platform.

## Getting started as a client

Freelegal connects individuals who need legal help with qualified lawyers and law firms willing to provide pro bono services. Whether you're facing a housing issue, family law matter, or other legal challenge, our platform makes it easy to submit your case and get matched with the right help.

## Registering as a client

While you can submit a case without creating an account, registering allows you to track your case status and receive updates.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the registration page">
    Visit the Freelegal website and click on the "Register" button in the navigation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select client as your user type">
    On the registration form, you'll see options for different user types. Select "Client" if you're seeking legal help.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Fill in your information">
    Provide your:
    - Full name
    - Email address
    - Password (and confirm it)
    - Phone number
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete registration">
    Click the "Register" button to create your account. You can now submit cases and track them through your account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  You don't need an account to submit a case. You can submit anonymously or with just your contact information.
</Note>

## Submitting a case

There are two ways to submit a case on Freelegal: identified submission (with contact details) or anonymous submission.

### Identified submission

This is the recommended approach as it allows legal representatives and account managers to contact you directly about your case.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the submission page">
    Navigate to the case submission page by clicking "Submit a Case" in the main navigation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Provide case details">
    Fill out the case submission form with:
    - **Case Title**: A brief, descriptive title for your legal issue
    - **Legal Category**: Select from options including:
      - Abuse
      - Family Law
      - Immigration
      - Housing/Tenancy
      - Employment
      - Consumer Rights
      - Criminal Defense
      - Other
    - **Case Description**: Provide detailed information about your situation, including relevant facts, timelines, and the type of help you're seeking
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add your contact information">
    Provide:
    - Contact name
    - Phone number
    - Email address
    
    All fields marked with a red asterisk (*) are required.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit your case">
    Review your information and click "Submit Case". You'll receive a unique Case ID that you can use to reference your submission.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Include as much detail as possible in your case description. The more information you provide, the easier it is for legal representatives to understand your situation and determine if they can help.
</Tip>

### Anonymous submission

If you're concerned about privacy, you can submit your case anonymously. This is useful for sensitive situations where you may not feel comfortable sharing your identity initially.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select anonymous submission">
    On the case submission page, click the yellow button that says "Make an anonymous submission".
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Fill out case information">
    Provide the same case details as an identified submission:
    - Case Title
    - Legal Category
    - Case Description
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Optionally add contact details">
    You can choose to include:
    - Contact name (optional)
    - Phone number (optional)
    - Email address (optional)
    
    Providing at least one contact method allows legal representatives to reach you if they can help.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Acknowledge the privacy notice">
    Check the box acknowledging that submitting anonymously may limit the ability to follow up unless you provide contact information.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit your case">
    Click "Submit Case" to complete your anonymous submission. You'll receive a Case ID for reference.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  **When to use anonymous submission:**
  - You're dealing with a sensitive matter like domestic violence or abuse
  - You want to understand your options before sharing your identity
  - Privacy concerns make you hesitant to share personal information initially
  
  **When to use identified submission:**
  - You want direct communication with legal representatives
  - Time is critical and you need immediate follow-up
  - You're comfortable sharing your contact information
</Note>

## What happens after submission

Once you submit your case:

1. **Case is reviewed**: Account managers (law student volunteers) review your submission to understand the legal issue and determine appropriate matches.

2. **Case is listed**: Your case appears in the system for legal representatives to browse. Personal information is kept confidential.

3. **Legal representatives express interest**: Lawyers and law firms review available cases and can express interest in cases that match their expertise.

4. **Account managers assign cases**: Based on expressed interest and case requirements, account managers assign cases to appropriate legal representatives.

5. **You're contacted**: The assigned legal representative will reach out to you using the contact information you provided to discuss your case and next steps.

<Tip>
  Keep your Case ID handy. You may need it to reference your submission if you contact Freelegal support.
</Tip>

## Tracking your case

If you submitted your case with contact information, you can expect:

- **Initial contact**: A legal representative or account manager will reach out within a few business days
- **Status updates**: You'll be informed as your case progresses through review and assignment
- **Direct communication**: Once assigned, your legal representative will communicate with you directly about your case

## Privacy and confidentiality

Your information is protected:

- All case information is kept confidential
- Personal details are only shared with approved legal representatives and account managers
- Anonymous submissions keep your identity private unless you choose to share contact details
- Legal representatives are bound by attorney-client privilege once they take your case

## Need help?

If you have questions about your submission or need assistance:

- Contact the Freelegal support team through the platform
- Reference your Case ID when inquiring about your submission
- Reach out to your assigned legal representative directly if you've been contacted
