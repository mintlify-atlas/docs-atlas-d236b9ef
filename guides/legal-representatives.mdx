This guide is for lawyers and law firms who want to provide pro bono legal services through the Freelegal platform.

## What is a legal representative?

Legal representatives on Freelegal are:

- Licensed attorneys providing pro bono services
- Law firms with pro bono programs
- Solo practitioners willing to take on pro bono cases
- Legal organizations focused on public service

As a legal representative, you browse available cases, express interest in matters within your expertise, and provide legal assistance to clients in need.

<Note>
  All services provided through Freelegal are pro bono (free of charge) for clients. This is an opportunity to give back, build expertise, and contribute to access to justice.
</Note>

## Registering as a legal representative

You can register either as a law firm or as an individual attorney.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the registration page">
    Visit the Freelegal website and click "Register" in the navigation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Legal Representative">
    On the registration form, choose "Legal Representative" as your user type.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose firm or individual">
    Select whether you're registering as:
    - **Firm**: Representing a law firm or legal organization
    - **Individual**: Registering as a solo practitioner or individual attorney
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Fill in your information">
    Provide:
    - Full name
    - Email address
    - Password (and confirm it)
    - Firm name (if registering as a firm)
    
    All required fields are marked with a red asterisk (*).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete registration">
    Click "Register" to create your account. Once approved, you'll gain access to the Firm Dashboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Registering as a firm vs. individual:**
  
  Choose **Firm** if:
  - Multiple attorneys will handle cases under one account
  - You want your firm name displayed publicly
  - You're establishing a formal pro bono program
  
  Choose **Individual** if:
  - You're a solo practitioner
  - You want cases assigned to you personally
  - You're volunteering independently of your firm
</Tip>

## Accessing your dashboard

After logging in, you'll be directed to the Case Portal (Firm Dashboard). This is your hub for browsing and managing pro bono cases.

The dashboard has two main tabs:

- **Available Cases**: New case submissions you can review and express interest in
- **My Interested Cases**: Cases where you've expressed interest and are awaiting assignment or have been assigned to you

## Browsing available cases

The "Available Cases" tab shows all case submissions that are currently seeking legal representation.

For each case, you can see:

- **Case ID**: Unique identifier
- **Title**: Brief description of the legal issue
- **Category**: Type of legal matter (Family Law, Housing/Tenancy, Employment, etc.)
- **Date**: When the case was submitted
- **Status**: "New" (just submitted) or "In Progress" (under review)
- **Actions**: Buttons to view details and express interest

<Note>
  The available cases list updates in real-time as new cases are submitted and others are assigned. Check back regularly to see new opportunities.
</Note>

## Reviewing case details

Before expressing interest, review the full case details:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click View on a case">
    Click the "View" button next to any case to see the complete submission.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Read the case description">
    Review:
    - The client's description of their legal issue
    - Relevant facts and timeline
    - What type of help they're seeking
    - Any urgency indicators
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Assess if you can help">
    Consider:
    - Does this match your area of expertise?
    - Do you have capacity to take on this case?
    - Do you have the resources needed?
    - Are there any potential conflicts of interest?
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Decide whether to express interest">
    If the case is a good fit, you can express interest. If not, continue browsing other cases.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Evaluating cases:**
  
  Strong candidates for expressing interest:
  - Cases within your practice area or expertise
  - Matters you have time and resources to handle
  - Issues where you can make a meaningful impact
  
  Cases to think carefully about:
  - Complex matters outside your expertise (unless you're learning)
  - Cases requiring significant time commitment when you're already busy
  - Situations with potential conflicts of interest
</Tip>

## Expressing interest in cases

When you find a case you'd like to help with, express your interest.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the case details">
    Make sure you fully understand the legal issue and have confirmed you can help.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Express Interest">
    In the Available Cases tab, click the yellow "Express Interest" button next to the case.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm your interest">
    The case will move to your "My Interested Cases" tab and account managers will be notified of your interest.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Wait for assignment">
    An account manager will review expressed interest from all legal representatives and assign the case to the best match. You'll be notified if you're selected.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Expressing interest doesn't guarantee assignment. Account managers review all interested representatives and select the best fit for each client's needs.
</Note>

## Managing your interested cases

Cases you've expressed interest in appear in the "My Interested Cases" tab.

From this tab, you can:

- **View case details**: Review the case information again
- **Withdraw interest**: If circumstances change and you can no longer take the case, click "Withdraw Interest" to remove yourself from consideration
- **Track status**: See which cases have been assigned to you

### Withdrawing interest

If you need to withdraw interest:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to My Interested Cases">
    Navigate to the "My Interested Cases" tab on your dashboard.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Withdraw Interest">
    Find the case and click the "Withdraw Interest" button.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm withdrawal">
    The case will return to your "Available Cases" view. Account managers will be notified.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  It's better to withdraw interest promptly if you can't take a case rather than leaving account managers waiting. This helps clients get matched with available help faster.
</Tip>

## What happens after assignment

When an account manager assigns a case to you:

1. **You're notified**: You'll receive notification that the case has been assigned to you

2. **You receive contact information**: The client's contact details become available to you

3. **You initiate contact**: Reach out to the client using their preferred contact method (phone or email)

4. **You conduct intake**: Have an initial conversation to:
   - Introduce yourself and your firm
   - Get additional details about their situation
   - Explain your role and what you can help with
   - Discuss next steps and timeline

5. **You provide representation**: Proceed with providing legal services according to your standard pro bono practices

<Note>
  Once you're assigned a case, you have the same professional responsibilities as any client relationship, including attorney-client privilege, conflict checks, and duty of competent representation.
</Note>

## Understanding the leaderboard

The platform includes a leaderboard to recognize active participants and encourage pro bono work.

### How points are earned

You earn points for pro bono activities:

- **+25 points**: Expressing interest in a case
- **+50 points**: Each month of ongoing representation
- **+100 points**: Successfully completing a pro bono case
- **+10 points**: Referring a case to another lawyer when you can't take it

### Leaderboard rankings

The leaderboard shows:

- **Rank**: Your position among all legal representatives
- **Firm/Name**: Your firm or individual name
- **Points**: Total points earned
- **Cases**: Number of cases you've handled

<Tip>
  The leaderboard is a friendly way to recognize contributions to pro bono work. Top performers often gain reputation within the legal community and demonstrate commitment to public service.
</Tip>

## Best practices for legal representatives

### Be selective and realistic

- Only express interest in cases you can genuinely handle
- Consider your current workload before committing
- Be honest about your expertise and limitations

### Respond promptly

- Check the dashboard regularly for new cases
- Respond quickly when assigned a case
- Contact clients within 24-48 hours of assignment

### Communicate clearly

- Set clear expectations with clients about:
  - What you can and cannot help with
  - Timeline for your services
  - How they can reach you
  - Next steps in their case

### Maintain professionalism

- Treat pro bono cases with the same care as paying clients
- Maintain confidentiality and attorney-client privilege
- Follow ethical rules and professional standards
- Document your work appropriately

### Follow through

- Complete cases you've taken on
- Keep clients informed of progress
- If circumstances change and you can't continue, help transition the case

### Use the platform effectively

- Update your profile with areas of expertise
- Track your cases through the dashboard
- Use the leaderboard as motivation
- Provide feedback to improve the platform

<Tip>
  **Building your pro bono practice:**
  
  - Start with 1-2 cases to understand the time commitment
  - Gradually increase your involvement as you build capacity
  - Focus on areas where you can make the biggest impact
  - Use pro bono work to develop expertise in new practice areas
</Tip>

## Tips for successful case handling

### Initial client contact

When you first reach out:

- Introduce yourself warmly and professionally
- Confirm you received their case submission
- Explain how Freelegal works and your role
- Ask questions to understand their situation fully
- Schedule a follow-up call or meeting

### Case assessment

During your intake:

- Identify the core legal issues
- Determine urgency and any deadlines
- Assess what services you can provide
- Identify any limitations or scope boundaries
- Discuss realistic outcomes

### Scope management

Be clear about scope:

- Define what you will and won't do
- Set boundaries on time and resources
- Explain any limitations upfront
- Put scope in writing (engagement letter)

### Ongoing communication

Throughout representation:

- Keep clients updated regularly
- Return calls and emails promptly
- Explain legal concepts in plain language
- Prepare them for what comes next
- Be honest about challenges

## Troubleshooting and common questions

### What if I express interest but don't get assigned?

This is normal. Account managers consider multiple factors:

- Other representatives may be a better fit
- The representative has more relevant expertise
- Geographic considerations
- Current workload balance

Continue expressing interest in cases that match your skills.

### Can I contact a client before assignment?

No. Client contact information is only provided after official assignment by an account manager. This protects client privacy and ensures orderly case management.

### What if a case is more complex than expected?

Be honest:

- Contact the account manager immediately
- Explain the complexity and your concerns
- Work together to find a solution
- Consider co-counsel or referral options
- Don't abandon the client without a transition plan

### How do I refer a case I can't handle?

If you've expressed interest but realize you can't take the case:

- Withdraw your interest promptly
- If you know another lawyer who can help, suggest them to the account manager
- You earn +10 points for helpful referrals

### Can I handle multiple cases at once?

Yes, but be realistic about your capacity. It's better to do a few cases well than to take on too many and provide inadequate service.
