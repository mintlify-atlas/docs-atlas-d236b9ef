Find answers to common questions about Freelegal, our pro bono legal matching platform.

## About Freelegal

<Accordion title="What is Freelegal?">
  Freelegal is the premier platform that seamlessly connects clients seeking legal representation with qualified law firms and expert account managers. Our intelligent system ensures the perfect match between clients and legal expertise for pro bono services. The platform brings together three key groups to widen access to justice: clients in need, law student volunteers (account managers), and legal representatives.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Who can use Freelegal?">
  Freelegal serves three main groups:
  
  - **Clients in need**: Individuals seeking free legal support with no barriers or fees
  - **Law students**: Volunteers who review cases and prepare them for matching with legal representatives
  - **Legal representatives**: Law firms and lawyers who provide pro bono services for suitable cases and earn recognition through our leaderboard system
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Is the service really free?">
  Yes. Freelegal connects clients with pro bono legal services at no cost. Our tagline is "Justice, Made Accessible" - we provide access to free legal support with no barriers and no fees.
</Accordion>

## Submissions and privacy

<Accordion title="How does anonymous submission work?">
  Freelegal offers two submission options:
  
  - **Standard submission**: Provide your contact information (name, phone, email) so we can stay in touch about your case
  - **Anonymous submission**: Submit your case without sharing personal information. You can optionally provide contact details if you choose
  
  When submitting anonymously, you only need to provide:
  - Case title
  - Legal category
  - Case description
  
  Contact information (name, phone, email) is optional. Note that submitting anonymously may limit our ability to follow up unless you provide contact info.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How is my privacy protected?">
  Your information is kept confidential and will only be shared with approved legal representatives and account managers. We take privacy seriously and ensure that your case details are only accessible to authorized users who can help with your legal needs.
</Accordion>

## Legal categories and cases

<Accordion title="What legal categories are supported?">
  Freelegal supports the following legal categories:
  
  - **Abuse**: Cases involving abuse situations
  - **Family Law**: Domestic violence restraining orders and other family law matters
  - **Immigration**: Immigration-related legal issues
  - **Housing/Tenancy**: Wrongful eviction, tenant rights, and housing disputes
  - **Employment**: Wage theft, workplace disputes, and employment issues
  - **Consumer Rights**: Consumer fraud and consumer protection cases
  - **Criminal Defense**: Criminal defense matters
  - **Other**: Legal issues that don't fit into the above categories
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens after I submit a case?">
  After you submit your case:
  
  1. **Submission confirmation**: You receive a unique Case ID for tracking
  2. **Account manager review**: Law student volunteers review your case and prepare it for matching
  3. **Matching process**: Your case is made available to legal representatives in the case portal
  4. **Legal representative interest**: Law firms can view and express interest in taking your case
  5. **Connection**: You're connected with the legal representative who can help with your specific needs
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How long does case review take?">
  Case review times vary depending on the complexity of your legal issue and the availability of account managers and legal representatives. Once your case is submitted, it will be reviewed by law student volunteers who prepare it for matching with appropriate legal representatives.
</Accordion>

## Leaderboard and recognition

<Accordion title="How does the leaderboard work?">
  The leaderboard recognizes law firms and legal representatives for their pro bono contributions. Firms earn points based on their activities:
  
  - **+100 points**: Successfully completing a pro bono case
  - **+25 points**: Expressing interest in a case
  - **+50 points**: Each month of ongoing representation
  - **+10 points**: For each referral to another lawyer when you can't take a case
  
  The leaderboard displays firm rankings based on total points earned and number of cases handled, creating recognition and friendly competition among legal representatives providing pro bono services.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can firms withdraw interest from cases?">
  Yes. Both law firms and account managers can manage case assignments:
  
  - **Firms**: Can withdraw interest from cases they've expressed interest in
  - **Account managers**: Can withdraw assignments from cases they've assigned to firms
  
  This flexibility ensures that cases are only handled by legal representatives who have the capacity and appropriate expertise.
</Accordion>
