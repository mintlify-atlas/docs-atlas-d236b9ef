## How to report issues

If you encounter any technical issues or bugs while using Freelegal:

1. **Check the FAQ**: Review our [Frequently Asked Questions](/resources/faq) to see if your issue is already addressed

2. **GitHub Issues**: Report bugs or technical problems on our [GitHub repository](https://github.com/WindowsM16a/freelegal-web/issues)

3. **Provide details**: When reporting issues, please include:
   - What you were trying to do
   - What happened instead
   - Your role (client, account manager, or legal representative)
   - Any error messages you received
   - Steps to reproduce the issue

<Note>
  For case-specific questions or concerns about your legal matter, please contact the account manager or legal representative assigned to your case directly.
</Note>

## Support by user type

### For clients

If you're seeking legal help:

- **Submitting cases**: Visit the [FAQ](/resources/faq) for information on how to submit cases, including anonymous submissions
- **Privacy concerns**: Learn about how your information is protected in our privacy section
- **Case status**: Use your unique Case ID (provided after submission) to track your case
- **Legal categories**: Review the supported legal categories to ensure your case fits our service areas

### For account managers

<Info>
  Account managers are law student volunteers who review cases and prepare them for matching with legal representatives.
</Info>

As an account manager, you can:

- **Review cases**: Access the case portal to review submitted pro bono cases
- **Assign cases**: Match cases with appropriate legal representatives based on expertise and availability
- **Manage assignments**: Withdraw assignments when necessary
- **Track cases**: Monitor case status (New, In Progress) through the dashboard

### For legal representatives

<Info>
  Legal representatives (law firms and lawyers) provide pro bono services and earn recognition through our leaderboard system.
</Info>

As a legal representative, you can:

- **Browse cases**: View available pro bono cases in the case portal
- **Express interest**: Indicate your interest in taking specific cases
- **Withdraw interest**: Remove your interest if you can't take a case
- **Earn recognition**: Gain points on the leaderboard for your pro bono contributions
- **Track your cases**: View cases you've expressed interest in through your dashboard

## Point system for legal representatives

Legal representatives earn recognition points for their pro bono work:

- **+100 points**: Successfully completing a pro bono case
- **+25 points**: Expressing interest in a case
- **+50 points**: Each month of ongoing representation
- **+10 points**: For each referral to another lawyer when you can't take a case

The leaderboard displays firm rankings based on total points and number of cases handled.

## Community guidelines

Freelegal is built on the principle that everyone deserves access to justice. When using the platform:

### For all users

- **Be respectful**: Treat all users with dignity and respect
- **Maintain confidentiality**: Keep case information confidential and secure
- **Communicate clearly**: Provide accurate and complete information
- **Act professionally**: Uphold the highest standards of professional conduct

### For legal representatives

- **Commitment to pro bono**: Only express interest in cases you can realistically take
- **Timely response**: Respond promptly to case assignments and client communications
- **Quality service**: Provide the same quality of service as paid clients would receive
- **Ethical standards**: Adhere to all legal and ethical obligations
- **Referrals**: When you can't take a case, help by referring it to other qualified lawyers

### For account managers

- **Thorough review**: Carefully review case submissions for completeness and clarity
- **Fair matching**: Match cases with legal representatives based on expertise and capacity
- **Confidentiality**: Protect client privacy and sensitive information
- **Professionalism**: Maintain professional communication with both clients and legal representatives

<Note>
  By using Freelegal, all users agree to uphold these community guidelines and contribute to making legal services more accessible.
</Note>
